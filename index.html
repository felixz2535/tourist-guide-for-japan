General Guide For Japan

Get Started

Introduction

Prologue

Japan is considerably an interesting country to visit. There are a huge range of activities that really can appeals to anyone and as well as very appetizing food to explore. As a tourist there is just too many things not to do. What the purpose of this website is to set the scope of what you can do in Japan, just note that this doesn’t include all of japan because there is just way too much.

Essential

If this is anything that you really need to do is learn a bit of Japanese when you go there. Just the basic not too much. Greetings realty important to set the right atmosphere. This can help you in a lot of situations and also help you connect with the locals.

Why Japan?

For starters Japan really diversifies from countryside to city. What that means is that you can choose between a clean and quiet environment to bond with the surrounding in rural areas or a lively city that changes as you explore it’s districts in the more urban areas. The cuisine is whole other topic to explore.

Seasons

Spring

When it comes to spring there is no better activities than cherry blossom viewing. People would gather near cherry blossom trees and have a picnic. You get to spent a great time with your friends, family, or co-workers. Since Golden Week starts from late April to early May it might be really expensive traveling around, so choose your plans wisely.

Summer

Often in the summer many family would go on vacations. Their destination might be beaches, amusement park really anything. Something that happens quite frequently in Japan is summer festivals. There are oftens stands that sells food or offers game with rewards. During these festival you might even be able to see fireworks!!





Fall

Fall is a really refreshing season with the autumn leaves turning red and orange, really a beautiful sight. People might rake up the leaves and have some sweet potato. Scenery in that time is really alluring, so might as well go to the country sides just for the scenery.

Winter

When it comes to winter you might think of christmas. Your first Christmas in Japan might be weird experience, looking around you would see couple hanging out and people buying KFC and strawberry short cake for their Christmas meals. Around the end of the year comes the new year in which Japanese people do celebrate. They often stay up late to count down to the New Year or climb a mountain to see the first sunrise. And on New Year people often eat Osechi Ryori which is kind of like a bento fill with a lot of dishes that each holds a specific meaning.
